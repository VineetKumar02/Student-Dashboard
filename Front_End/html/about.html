What is GPA and CGPA?

  • Grade Point Average (GPA) is the average of grade points obtained in one(single) semester.

  • Cumulative grade point average (CGPA) is the average grade point obtained by the students at the end of their academic program.

Difference between GPA and CGPA

Although both GPA and CGPA indicate the student's academic performance, one big difference exists between them. GPA is the average of the student's grade points in all the courses during a single semester, while CGPA is the average of the grade points obtained by the student during the whole course. GPA is calculated for a single semester, while CGPA is calculated for the entire course duration.

Grading System at SSN

The student's performance is reported using letter grades, each carrying specific points as shown below.

Grade Letters Grade Points Marks range
O (Outstanding) 10 91-100
A+ (Excellent) 9 81-90
A (Very good) 8 71-80
B+ (Good) 7 61-70
B (Average) 6 51-60
C (Satisfactory) 5 < 50
RA (Re-Appearance) 0 -
SA (shortage of attendance) 0 -
W (Withdrawal) 0 -

Note: To get distinction, students should get no less than 8.5 CGPA score without any arrears/backlogs in all the semesters. Students should score at least 6.5 CGPA to get First class


The course's assessment is evaluated on a total marks basis. If the student obtains any of these grades (O, A+, A, B+, or B), then the student is deemed to have passed a particular course. After the declaration of results, students will be issued Grade Sheets containing the following details:

  • The college where the student has studied.
  • The list of courses enrolled and the grades scored.
  • The GPA for the semester.
  • The CGPA of all courses enrolled from the first semester onwards.

CGPA to Percentage Conversion

CGPA score can be converted to percentage by multiplying the CGPA value with 10.


Percentage = CGPA x 10

For example, if the CGPA score is 9.83
Percentage = 9.83 x 10 = 98.3%
